# Champion Community Launch Kit

## Objective

Design a champion network that accelerates adoption, localizes learning, and absorbs first-line questions before central support becomes the bottleneck.

## Champion model

- one champion per 50 to 80 active users
- one community lead per region
- one shared FAQ and escalation backlog
- one monthly story of value from the field

## Champion responsibilities

- run office hours and local demos
- collect prompt-quality issues and support trends
- model good behavior in front of peers
- redirect unsafe or out-of-scope requests
- keep a simple log of top blockers and wins

## Scale pattern for 1,000+ users

- recruit by manager nomination and performance credibility
- give every champion the same starter pack
- group them by timezone and business function
- publish one digest each month with wins, FAQs, and upcoming priorities

## Minimum starter pack

- role-based prompt examples
- approved data and human review boundaries
- FAQ and escalation matrix
- office hours facilitation guide
- feedback form and weekly summary template

## Operating cadence

- weekly office hours
- weekly champion sync
- biweekly FAQ refresh
- monthly spotlight and sponsor readout

## Metrics

- champion attendance rate
- questions solved by champions
- repeat use after training
- sentiment from office hours
- issue escalation closure time

## Interview line

"I like champions because they create local trust, not because they are a free support channel. If I cannot tell you how champions are recruited, trained, measured, and supported, then I do not really have a champions program."
