# Feedback and Support Operating Model

## Objective

Make Copilot adoption sustainable by defining how people get help, how feedback is captured, and how blockers are removed quickly.

## Tiered support

- **Tier 0**: in-product guidance, FAQs, prompt examples
- **Tier 1**: champion office hours and peer support
- **Tier 2**: central adoption helpdesk for access, quality, and launch issues
- **Tier 3**: security, compliance, and policy escalation

## Feedback sources

- post-training survey
- office hours notes
- helpdesk ticket themes
- champion community digests
- manager retrospective comments

## Weekly operating rhythm

1. Review top ticket and feedback themes.
2. Identify what can be solved by training, communications, or product guidance.
3. Assign blocker owners from adoption, support, or platform teams.
4. Confirm what goes into the steering committee readout.

## What good looks like

- users know where to go for help
- champions close routine questions fast
- reopened tickets decline over time
- repeated themes drive visible content updates
- adoption and success teams remove blockers before trust drops

## Interview line

"I treat support as part of the adoption system, not a cleanup function after launch. If I cannot explain the feedback loop and blocker-removal rhythm, then the rollout is not ready to scale."
